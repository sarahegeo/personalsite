Example Analysis

Sarah George

Overview

In the 1990’s the Spruce Beetle (Dendroctonus rufipennis) wreaked havoc on the spruce trees of the Kenai Peninsula in South Central Alaska. Warmer temperatures and prolonged drought conditions enabled the beetle population to surge above regular numbers. This outbreak, peaking in 1996, affected over 1.6 million acres of spruce forest. The landscape still bears the scars of this disturbance in shifts from dense forest to grasslands and younger forests. This project aims to investigate post-disturbance forest recovery.

Data & methods

Tree location and dbh data was collected for every tree in 10m diamter plots across the spruce forest at Inspiration Ridge Preserve in Homer, AK. We first plot a histogram of dbh’s corrected for area to determine what stage of recovery the forest is in. Then we investigate the distribution of trees within Stand #10. We first extract the trees from Stand #10 and visualize the distribution of different dbh sizes.

Analysis

hist(treepoints$DBH,
     main = "Histogram of DBH Across all Stands",
     xlab = "DBH (mm)",
     ylab = "Frequency",
     col = "forestgreen",
     border = "gray10")

Figure 2: Histogram showing DBH class frequencies in mm for all sampled trees. Histogram shows a right skew which indicates stands are in the stand initiation phase post-disturbance. 

#plot all of the tree points/stands
plot(treepoints$Long, treepoints$Lat,
     main = "Individual Tree Points",
     xlab = "Longitude",
     ylab = "Latitude")

#extract stand 10 points
#we chose stand 10 because it has the most observations (76)
stand10 <- treepoints[treepoints$Stand_ID == 10, ]
#head(stand10)

#turn df into shapefile and assign CRS
stand10_sf <- st_as_sf(stand10, coords = c("Long", "Lat"), crs = 4326)
#transform crs to ESPG::3338, NAD83 Alaska
stand10_sf <- st_transform(stand10_sf, 3338)

#plot Stand 10 to see the points
ggplot() +
  geom_sf(data = stand10_sf, color = "forestgreen", size = 2) +
  theme_minimal() +
  labs(title = "Tree Locations – Stand 10")

#visualization with dbh graduated symbols
tm_shape(stand10_sf) +
  tm_symbols(
    size="DBH",
    fill="#4d8f74",
    fill.legend =
      tm_legend(
        size.scale = tm_scale_continuous(values.scale = 0.75))) +
  tm_grid(
    labels.size = 0.4,
    n.x=3,
    n.y=5) +
  tm_title(text = "Distribution of DBH (mm) in Stand 10",
         fontface = "bold")

Results

The histogram distribution is consistent with a forest in recovery after a disturbance. This is in line with our hypothesis that the forest is still affected by the bark beetle outbreak in the 90’s. We are curious to see if there are any spatial patterns in the reforestation of this area.

Reflection

The next step in this project is to conduct a spatial point pattern analysis to test for clustering and create summary tables of each stand. The major limit of this project is that we do not have the location of every tree in the forest. Because of the size of the land and the limited time to collect field measurements we were only able to do 10 stands. Ideally we would’ve been able to do an entire census of the trees.
